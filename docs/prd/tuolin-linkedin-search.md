## Problem Statement

Tuolin currently has a LinkedIn content workflow, but it does not provide a controlled way to turn product-related LinkedIn posts into reviewed sales-prospect candidates and then send connection invitations through the operator's already signed-in LinkedIn account.

The business needs a reusable, product-grounded workflow that starts from user-confirmed search keywords, searches LinkedIn posts, identifies posts that may represent prospective customers or channel partners, resolves one appropriate person per relevant company, shows the post and author/contact evidence to the user, and sends connection invitations only after explicit batch confirmation.

The workflow must remain under human control. It must not guess unsupported product facts, silently broaden search criteria, add unreviewed candidates, switch accounts, remove an approved invitation note, resume after a platform restriction, or repeat invitations across tasks. It also needs durable operational state because different runs and keywords can surface the same post, company, or person.

The first release is intentionally pragmatic: an invitation confirmed as submitted by LinkedIn is considered a successful result. Measuring lead quality, acceptance rate, conversion, or whether every selected person is the ideal buyer is deferred to later versions.

## Solution

Create an independent `$tuolin-linkedin-search` skill for Tuolin product-led LinkedIn prospect discovery and connection-invitation dispatch.

Each task binds to one formal Tuolin product from the verified Agent interface and one visible, already authenticated LinkedIn member identity. The skill asks only unresolved business questions that map to controls LinkedIn post search or the approved invitation batch can actually use. Questions are asked one at a time in Chinese, with exactly one recommended answer and a reason, ending with `是否确认？`; a plain `确认` confirms only the current question.

After the interview, Codex uses the official Codex Chrome extension and the operator's existing signed-in Chrome profile to search LinkedIn Posts by the confirmed, ordered English keywords. It evaluates visible post content with a binary `Continue` or `Skip` decision and one reason. Relevant posts must concern selling, promoting, distributing, branding, sourcing, or potentially reselling/private-labeling the target product category. The workflow then resolves one eligible person per company, preferring owner/founder, procurement/sourcing, general management, and product-management roles in that order.

Before any invitation is sent, Codex prints and persists candidate cards containing the source keyword, post text and URL, relevance reason, author/company data, selected member name/title/company/profile URL, and the planned note behavior. The user reviews one batch, may remove candidates, and explicitly confirms the closed batch. The confirmed batch is not automatically replenished.

Invitations are sent sequentially with the user-confirmed fixed interval, defaulting to five minutes. The requested per-run ceiling defaults to 10. A shared, account-scoped Prospecting Contact Ledger prevents duplicate cards and duplicate sends across runs and enforces a local rolling limit of no more than 100 invitation-dispatch successes recorded by this skill in any 168-hour window. Before every send, the workflow revalidates the account, candidate state, deduplication state, and remaining capacity.

The skill uses deterministic modules for run state, deduplication, limits, transition rules, reporting, and recovery, while Codex performs the business interview, semantic post judgment, contact selection, and visible Chrome interaction. Failures and ambiguous states are recorded explicitly. Platform restrictions stop the batch immediately and require a new authorized task; ordinary recoverable interruptions may continue the same run only after reconciliation and fresh final authorization.

## Implementation Decisions

- Add a new independent `$tuolin-linkedin-search` skill. Keep the existing LinkedIn campaign/content skill unchanged in responsibility.
- Add a dedicated `linkedin_search` downstream context sourced only from the verified Agent interface. It exposes official product identity, approved external names, application scenarios, and sales-use terms; it never reads raw knowledge, campaign outputs, or prospecting artifacts as formal facts.
- Resolve one canonical product per run. Product resolution is a prerequisite state; unresolved or unverified products block the workflow and hand off to the knowledge-base agent.
- Model the workflow as a durable state machine with explicit stages for product resolution, interview, account binding, discovery, candidate review, closed-batch authorization, dispatch, reconciliation, completion, and stopped/blocked outcomes.
- Extract a deep `Prospecting Policy Engine` with a small deterministic interface. It owns candidate eligibility states, company/person deduplication, account-scoped reservation, rolling-cap calculations, interval gates, allowed transitions, and stop/recovery rules.
- Extract a deep `Prospecting Run Store` responsible for isolated run creation, atomic state transitions, human-readable and machine-readable artifacts, authorization records, reports, and safe restart loading.
- Extract a shared `Prospecting Contact Ledger` scoped by normalized LinkedIn account identity. It stores normalized member, company, and source-post keys plus dispatch status and timestamps. A success visible in LinkedIn must be durably recorded before execution may continue.
- Add an account-scoped active-run lock. The lock prevents concurrent discovery/dispatch runs for the same LinkedIn identity and is reconciled rather than blindly overwritten after interruption.
- Add a `Knowledge Context Provider` that validates Agent-interface freshness and resolves the one product-bound `linkedin_search` context consumed by interview and note generation.
- Add a focused interview component that derives only missing questions from supported LinkedIn post filters and external-action choices. It enforces one question per turn, one recommendation with reason, current-question-only confirmation, and no bulk remaining confirmation.
- Keep user-provided market terms in run state as `search_only` vocabulary. They can affect search queries and relevance judgment but cannot alter product facts or external claims.
- Add a `Candidate Discovery Pipeline` that iterates ordered keywords, caps opened posts per keyword, records `Continue`/`Skip` plus a reason, and never expands or repeats searches to fill a target count.
- Keep post relevance as an AI semantic decision behind a narrow interface. The deterministic layer validates that a decision and reason exist but does not invent a quality score.
- Add a `Contact Resolver` that handles individual-authored and company-authored posts, applies the confirmed role priority, selects at most one member per company, and yields an explicit skip reason when no standard Connect path exists.
- Add a `Candidate Review Renderer` that produces matching Markdown and JSON candidate cards and a final authorization brief. Screenshots are opt-in evidence, not default artifacts.
- Treat the confirmed candidate list as an immutable `Closed Candidate Batch`. Removing a candidate is allowed before confirmation; replacement/backfill after confirmation is not.
- Add an optional `Invitation Note Policy`. It produces one short English draft, requires explicit confirmation or edit, freezes the exact text for the batch, and forces a new choice if LinkedIn cannot send the note as authorized.
- Add a small `Chrome Prospecting Adapter` over the official Codex Chrome extension. Its interface covers account identity, post search/filter controls, visible post/profile extraction, connection-state inspection, invitation submission, and success/error observation.
- The Chrome adapter prefers structured DOM interaction and can use screenshot/vision fallback. It does not own login credentials, launch a headless browser, implement fingerprint evasion, or promise avoidance of LinkedIn enforcement.
- Define `Invitation Dispatch Success` only when the LinkedIn UI visibly confirms the invitation was sent. Acceptance by the recipient is not required for first-release success.
- Before showing a candidate and again before sending, reconcile the local ledger with visible LinkedIn state. Ambiguous state is a skip/stop condition, never permission to retry.
- Enforce a default per-run ceiling of 10 and an account-scoped local rolling ceiling of 100 successful dispatches over 168 hours. The effective run ceiling is the smaller of requested and remaining capacity and is recalculated before every send.
- Treat the rolling ceiling as a local policy based only on actions recorded by this skill. Surface that manual LinkedIn actions are outside the count; do not describe it as an official LinkedIn limit.
- Use a user-confirmed fixed interval, recommending five minutes. Do not add randomized delays or frame them as human simulation.
- Distinguish candidate-local failures from platform-level stops. Candidate-local failures may continue; restrictions, CAPTCHA, security checkpoints, logout, or repeated unexplained failures stop the full batch immediately.
- Require a new task and fresh authorization after a platform-level stop. Allow same-run recovery after ordinary interruption only through account revalidation, last-action reconciliation, remaining-batch display, and renewed dispatch authorization.
- Integrate natural-language routing so explicit LinkedIn prospect-search requests select the new skill without colliding with campaign-planning, image, review, or publishing intents.
- Package the new skill and supporting deterministic runtime consistently with the marketplace's source and plugin distribution structure.
- Keep prospecting artifacts in generated operational reports and the shared ledger, outside the formal knowledge corpus. Formal market-intelligence promotion remains a separate knowledge-base operation.
- Record explicit warnings that LinkedIn may restrict automated or unusual activity and that the feature makes no stealth, compliance, or account-safety guarantee.

## Testing Decisions

- Tests will assert externally observable behavior, persisted contracts, and state transitions rather than private helper calls, exact internal decomposition, or brittle LinkedIn markup.
- Unit-test the Prospecting Policy Engine as a pure module: per-run ceilings, rolling 168-hour boundary behavior, account scoping, successful-send counting, remaining-capacity calculation, candidate/company deduplication, reservations, retry eligibility, transition legality, and platform-stop rules.
- Unit-test URL and identity normalization with multiple LinkedIn URL variants, missing identifiers, redirects, and ambiguous collisions.
- Unit-test the Prospecting Contact Ledger and active-run lock with temporary storage: atomic writes, cross-run duplicate suppression, one-contact-per-company behavior, stale-lock reconciliation, and fail-closed behavior when a confirmed external action cannot be recorded.
- Unit-test the interview contract: one unresolved question per turn, exactly one recommendation and reason, `确认` applying only to the current question, omission of already known answers, exclusion of unsupported geography, and optional filters asked only when relevant.
- Unit-test keyword handling so AI suggestions use verified product context while user market terms remain search-only and cannot mutate formal product identity or claims.
- Unit-test candidate-card and final-brief rendering so Markdown and JSON contain the same required evidence, account identity, note decision, limit calculation, interval, approval state, and final outcome.
- Unit-test immutable closed-batch behavior: removals before confirmation are honored, no backfill occurs, and recovery cannot add newly discovered candidates.
- Contract-test the Chrome Prospecting Adapter against deterministic fake browser observations instead of live LinkedIn in standard CI. Cover signed-in identity, account change, Posts filter application, Connect availability, existing pending/connected state, note availability, success confirmation, ambiguous result, CAPTCHA, logout, and restriction states.
- Integration-test the product-context boundary against a temporary verified Agent interface: one product resolves, stale/missing contexts block, raw files are not read, and `search_only` terms do not become formal knowledge.
- Integration-test ordered multi-keyword discovery with recorded browser fixtures: 50-opened-post ceiling per keyword, Continue/Skip reasons, normal exhaustion, no criteria broadening, and no repeated search to fill the run ceiling.
- Integration-test individual-author and company-author contact resolution, including role priority, one contact per company, no eligible role, and absence of a standard Connect path.
- Integration-test pre-card and pre-send reconciliation using ledger plus fake live state, including manual invitation detection and the success-visible/ledger-write-failure stop case.
- Integration-test sequential dispatch with a controllable clock: exact fixed interval, cap recalculation before each send, candidate-local continuation, platform-level immediate stop, and accurate succeeded/skipped/failed/unexecuted reporting.
- Integration-test ordinary interruption recovery: reload durable state, revalidate account, reconcile the last candidate, render the remaining closed batch, require fresh authorization, and prohibit new discovery/backfill.
- Integration-test platform-stop restart behavior: the old run remains incomplete, remaining candidates are preserved, and a new authorized task is required.
- Integration-test natural-language routing so the new prospect-search intent does not intercept existing LinkedIn campaign planning, content confirmation, image, review, or publishing requests.
- Add packaging tests ensuring the source skill, plugin-distributed skill, runtime modules, and entrypoints remain synchronized and installable.
- Reuse the existing LinkedIn agent test style for project fixtures and natural-language routing, the downstream-context tests for Agent-interface boundaries, and the video-creation tests as prior art for isolated run directories, state machines, confirmation gates, and recovery.
- Add a manual acceptance checklist using the official Codex Chrome extension. First run a read-only smoke test that binds the visible account, applies one keyword, inspects posts, and renders at least one candidate card. Run a real invitation smoke test only after a human reviews the exact candidate and explicitly authorizes that external action.
- Manual acceptance must verify that no credentials or browser session data enter run artifacts, that the visible account matches the final brief, and that a successfully submitted invitation appears in both LinkedIn state and the shared ledger.

## Out of Scope

- LinkedIn People search as the primary discovery route.
- Geography filtering for Posts search when the visible LinkedIn interface does not offer it.
- Lead scoring, confidence scores, acceptance-rate metrics, conversion tracking, ROI measurement, or automatic optimization of candidate fit.
- Automatic messaging after connection acceptance, InMail, email outreach, Follow fallback, or scraping contact details.
- Multiple contacts per company or automatically replacing an individual post author with a senior employee.
- Competitor-intelligence collection that sends connection invitations.
- Automatic keyword expansion, automatic filter relaxation, repeated searches, or candidate backfill to reach a requested count.
- Per-candidate personalized invitation-note rewriting during batch execution.
- Unattended recurring schedules, daily background operation, or 24/7 execution.
- Automatic resume after a platform restriction, CAPTCHA, security checkpoint, or logout.
- Repository-owned headless Chrome, full-CDP automation, browser-fingerprint spoofing, IP tunneling, stealth/evasion features, or guarantees against LinkedIn detection/restriction.
- Managing LinkedIn passwords, OTPs, cookies, local storage, or login recovery.
- Treating the local 100-per-168-hour ceiling as an official LinkedIn rule or counting manual invitations performed outside the skill.
- Writing candidate observations directly into formal product knowledge or market intelligence.
- Modifying the existing LinkedIn campaign-planning and content-publication skill to perform prospecting.
- Improving failed generated results in place through silent rewind, hidden resubmission, or alteration of a confirmed batch.

## Further Notes

- This PRD records a first-release business rule: a visibly confirmed invitation submission is the success event. Whether the invitation is accepted and whether the person is the ideal prospect are later optimization concerns.
- The agreed default is a run ceiling of 10 invitations, with a local account-scoped rolling ceiling of 100 successes over 168 hours and a recommended fixed five-minute interval. These values are operational product requirements, not claims about LinkedIn's official limits.
- The workflow uses the official Codex Chrome extension because the operator explicitly selected the existing signed-in Chrome profile as the browser boundary.
- The user acknowledged platform/account risk during requirements discovery. The implementation must still fail loudly and must not market the workflow as undetectable, platform-approved, or safe from restrictions.
- Existing architectural decisions already establish three boundaries that this PRD follows: prospect search is a separate skill, browser operation uses the official Codex Chrome extension, and product grounding uses a dedicated `linkedin_search` Agent-interface context.
- The issue should enter normal maintainer review with the `enhancement` category and `needs-triage` state.
